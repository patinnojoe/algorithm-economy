Algorithmic Economy:
A Data Analysis of Social Media Algorithms in the Age of Monetized Content

## Background

Introduce the concept of the Algorithmic Economy.
Social media platforms have transformed attention into a valuable economic resource. Creators, businesses, politicians, and influencers increasingly depend on visibility, engagement, and audience reach to generate income and influence.

This project introduces the concept of the Algorithmic Economy, defined as a digital ecosystem in which platform algorithms allocate visibility and that visibility can be converted into economic and social value.

Using a dataset of YouTube Shorts and TikTok content, this project investigates the characteristics associated with engagement and virality and explores whether algorithmic success can be predicted using machine learning techniques.

## Research Problem

In traditional economies, access to capital and productive resources often determines economic outcomes.
In social media ecosystems, visibility functions as a form of capital.
The central question of this project is:
What characteristics are associated with higher engagement and virality in the Algorithmic Economy?

## Research Objectives

The project seeks to:

1. Identify content categories that generate the highest engagement.
2. Compare engagement patterns across TikTok and YouTube Shorts.
3. Examine the influence of creator size on content performance.
4. Investigate which content characteristics are associated with virality.
5. Build machine learning models capable of predicting algorithmic success.
6. Interpret findings through the lens of the Algorithmic Economy.
